## What you asked for

How Agentic AI — autonomous systems that plan, use tools, and loop on feedback without constant human prompting — is transforming Industrial AI across manufacturing, energy, logistics, oil and gas, and pharmaceuticals, and what the current state of adoption, real-world use cases, technology stacks, and deployment barriers looks like as of mid-2026.

## What we built

## Headline findings

1. **Supply chain is the most mature production vertical** — General Mills autonomously assesses 5,000+ daily shipments and has saved $20M+ since FY2024; Gartner projects SCM software with agentic AI will grow from under $2B (2025) to $53B by 2030.

2. **Energy and O&G are accelerating fast** — Grid self-healing agents reduce outage durations by up to 80% in utility pilots; Aker BP/ABB/Cognite confirmed the first production-grade agent-to-agent orchestration deployment in O&G in May 2026; the energy/utilities agentic AI market is growing at a 36% CAGR.

3. **Pharma is moving carefully in "draft-and-review" mode** — 67% of life sciences firms ran agentic AI pilots as of Q1 2026, with 40–50% reductions in deviation investigation cycle times; no fully autonomous execution in GMP-regulated environments has been confirmed as of mid-2026.

4. **The gap between capability and production deployment is a verification problem, not a model problem** — An academic study of 12 industrial companies found only 1 operating at multi-agent orchestration level; 4 others had the capability but couldn't deploy it due to missing output-verification mechanisms. ISO/IEC TS 22440 — the first AI functional safety standard — is still in Committee Draft stage.

5. **Gartner predicts over 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by end of 2027** — Primary reasons are escalating costs, unclear business value, and inadequate risk controls; McKinsey separately reports only ~5.5% of companies get real AI ROI and fewer than 10% have scaled AI to measurable value.

## Limitations & caveats

- **Vendor-sourced performance claims are unverified.** Siemens' 25% maintenance reduction, XMPro's 30–40% mining productivity gains, and BCG's 30–70% EBIT potential for O&G are self-reported or consultant projections with no independent audit trail.
- **The "pilot vs. production" boundary is blurry.** For most case studies, the distinction between an extended pilot and limited production cannot be independently verified from public disclosures.
- **Market size estimates diverge significantly.** Different analyst firms report 2025/2026 agentic AI market bases that vary by 2–5x depending on scope definitions. All market figures should be read as directional order-of-magnitude, not precise forecasts.
- **Safety-critical environments (nuclear, chemical, aerospace) are nearly absent from public reporting.** This report's coverage skews toward verticals where vendors are publicly disclosing deployments; the most conservative regulated industries may be further behind than the data suggests.
- **ISO/IEC TS 22440 timeline is uncertain.** The standard is in Committee Draft stage; ratification and regulatory adoption could slip past 2027, which would extend the period during which human-in-the-loop is the only trusted verification mechanism.
- **EU AI Act enforcement is still under interpretation.** European industrial operators face planning uncertainty about exactly how process control agents will be classified and what conformity assessments will be required.
- **OT cybersecurity risk is underrepresented in vendor literature.** OT ransomware attacks grew 87% YoY in 2025 and agentic agents with write access to PLCs significantly expand the attack surface — a risk most vendor marketing materials minimize.
