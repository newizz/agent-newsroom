## Headline findings
1. **An LLM is fundamentally a next-token predictor.** Everything else — chat, summarisation, code — is that one trick applied many times.
2. **Tokens (not words) are the atomic unit.** ~4 characters = 1 token, on average.
3. **Attention is the post-2017 breakthrough.** Every token "looks at" every other to figure out context.
4. **Context windows are O(n²)** — that's the reason long-context models are expensive to serve.
5. **Sampling settings change personality, not knowledge.** Temperature/top-p just choose *how* to pick the next token from a fixed distribution.
