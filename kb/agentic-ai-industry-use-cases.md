## What you asked for

What are the most concrete, real-world deployed applications of agentic AI — autonomous systems that plan multi-step tasks, use tools, and iterate toward goals — across different industries today, and what measurable outcomes do they produce? Coverage spans eight industries: software engineering, customer service, healthcare, finance/banking, legal, manufacturing/supply chain, retail/e-commerce, and pharma/research.

## What we built

The dashboard is a data-story layout covering all eight industries, with KPI cards showing headline metrics (51% faster coding, 84% autonomous case resolution, 40% prior-auth without humans, 18 months vs. 4–6 years in drug discovery), an interactive industry maturity matrix, per-industry case cards with named deployments and workflow patterns, a metrics comparison table, and a timeline from 2017 to 2030. The key takeaway: software engineering and customer service are the most production-mature sectors, while pharma/research shows the most dramatic speed gains in documented pilots.

## Headline findings

1. **Software engineering leads in scale** — GitHub Copilot Agent Mode reached 15+ million developers by mid-2025; Stripe generates 1,000+ AI-merged pull requests per week; developers complete tasks 51% faster, and AI now accounts for roughly 46% of committed code for active Copilot users.

2. **Customer service is the widest agentic deployment by company count** — Salesforce Agentforce resolved 84% of support cases autonomously in Salesforce's own portal (1M+ conversations/year), closed 29,000+ enterprise deals by FY26, and completed 2.4 billion agentic work units; Finnair's agents autonomously handle 80% of customer questions.

3. **Healthcare admin is crossing into production; clinical is still pilot** — MUSC Health's prior-authorisation agents complete 40% of requests without human involvement; Sentara Health reclaimed thousands of nursing hours across 12 hospitals; clinical AI (diagnostics, treatment planning) remains under strict regulatory gates.

4. **Pharma shows the most dramatic time compression** — Insilico Medicine identified a novel IPF drug candidate in 18 months vs. the 4–6 year industry average at ~$150,000 for the discovery phase; 173+ AI-originated drug programs are in clinical development as of early 2026, up from ~24 in late 2023.

5. **The production gap is the biggest story** — Only 11–33% of enterprises (survey variance) have agentic AI in active production despite 69–79% claiming some form of adoption; Gartner forecasts that 40%+ of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by 2027, primarily due to legacy system incompatibility and organisational readiness gaps, not technical failure.

## Limitations & caveats

- **Survey variance on adoption**: "In production" figures range from 11% (Deloitte, strict definition) to 33% (Battery Ventures, looser definition). Treat all adoption percentages as directional.
- **Vendor-claimed metrics**: Several headline numbers — 60% fraud false-positive reduction (finance), 90% contract processing time reduction (legal), 20–30% hedging cost savings (finance) — come from single vendor sources without independent verification. They are flagged in the data table.
- **Healthcare clinical gap**: Production evidence for clinical agentic AI (diagnostics, autonomous treatment planning) is thin; the strongest healthcare data is administrative (prior auth, claims, scheduling).
- **Pharma "agentic" vs. ML ambiguity**: Not all 173+ AI-originated drug programs use agentic multi-step pipelines — some use earlier-generation ML molecular modelling. The "agentic" label is sometimes applied loosely across the industry.
- **Manufacturing independent verification**: Siemens' 50% productivity claim and the Walmart/H&M retail figures are single-source (Siemens press releases and one industry analyst blog); no independent confirmation found.
- **Rapid pace of change**: The agentic AI space is moving faster than any other enterprise technology category. Adoption statistics cited here carry a rough 6-month expiry assumption.
