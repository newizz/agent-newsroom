# Brief: The Story of a Cat's Life

**Slug:** cat-life-stories
**Created:** 2026-06-03
**Template hint:** timeline
**Ambiguity:** yellow
**Estimated depth:** standard

## Original prompt
> เล่าเรื่องราวชีวิตแมว

## Refined question
What does a cat's life look like from birth to old age — covering biological development, behavior, emotional world, and the human-cat relationship — presented in an engaging, narrative-driven format for a general audience?

## Scope
- **In scope:**
  - Life stages of a domestic cat: newborn, kitten, juvenile, adult, senior
  - Key developmental milestones at each stage (eyes opening, weaning, first heat, peak activity, aging signs)
  - Behavioral traits and instincts characteristic of each life phase
  - Emotional and social life: bonding, territory, play, communication
  - The human-cat relationship across a cat's lifespan
  - Average lifespan figures and factors that influence longevity
  - Interesting or lesser-known facts woven into the narrative

- **Out of scope:**
  - Wild or feral cat populations (focus is domestic cats)
  - Veterinary disease reference or medical treatment guides
  - Breed-by-breed differences (treat as a "typical domestic cat")
  - Cat nutrition or product recommendations
  - Historical domestication deep-dive (mention only as brief context)

## Audience
General public — cat owners, cat lovers, and the curious. Assumes no specialist knowledge. Readers care about understanding their cats better and finding the facts charming or surprising. Thai-speaking audience is likely primary, but content should be presented in a universally accessible way.

## Success criteria
A successful dashboard will:
- Guide the reader through a cat's entire lifespan in a clear, stage-by-stage narrative that feels like a story, not a textbook
- Highlight 5–8 surprising or delightful facts that make readers say "I didn't know that"
- Make the emotional arc of a cat's life (dependence → curiosity → independence → companionship → vulnerability) visible and relatable
- Work as a self-contained interactive experience requiring no prior cat knowledge

## Key questions Research must answer
1. What are the officially recognized life stages of a domestic cat, and what are the typical age ranges for each?
2. What are the most important behavioral and physical milestones within each stage (e.g., when do kittens open their eyes, when does adolescent roaming behavior peak)?
3. What is the average lifespan of a domestic cat, and which factors most strongly influence whether a cat lives 10 vs 20 years?
4. How do cats communicate — vocalizations, body language, scent — and how does this change across life stages?
5. What is known about cats' emotional and cognitive inner life: do they form attachments, do they grieve, do they recognize their owners?
6. What are the most common signs of aging in senior cats, and how does behavior shift in the final years?
7. What are 5–8 genuinely surprising facts about cat biology or behavior that most people do not know?

## Assumptions made
- Assumed domestic cat (Felis catus) rather than wild cats, because "แมว" in Thai almost always refers to the household pet.
- Assumed a general, emotionally engaging narrative tone rather than a scientific or veterinary reference format.
- Assumed standard depth (~8–12 min read equivalent) rather than a quick explainer, because a life-story arc benefits from breadth across all stages.
- Assumed the dashboard will be in English (or bilingual), consistent with the broader agent-newsroom project output conventions, even though the prompt was in Thai.

## Open risks
- Quantitative data (exact milestone ages, lifespan statistics) varies across sources; Research should cross-check and use ranges rather than single figures.
- "Story of cat life" could skew toward anecdotal listicles online — Research should prioritize veterinary or ethological sources over pop-science blogs.
- Emotional/cognitive claims about cats are an active research area; Research should flag where findings are preliminary.
