## Original prompt
> dog life

## Refined question
What does life look like from a dog's perspective — their daily routines, behavioral needs, sensory world, social bonds, and key life stages from puppy to senior?

## Scope
- **In scope:** dog daily routines and sleep patterns, social and pack behavior, sensory capabilities (smell, hearing, vision), life stages (puppy, adult, senior), play and exercise needs, communication (body language, vocalizations), bond with humans, common breeds and temperament differences
- **Out of scope:** dog health conditions and veterinary medicine in depth, dog training techniques (beyond basics), breed breeding/genetics, working/service dog specializations, wild dogs and wolves (kept to brief comparisons only)

## Audience
Curious general readers — dog owners, prospective owners, and animal lovers who want an engaging, insight-rich look at what it's actually like to be a dog. No specialist knowledge assumed; they care about interesting facts, "aha" moments, and practical insight they can connect to their own pet.

## Success criteria
A successful dashboard will:
- Make readers feel they truly understand a dog's inner world and daily experience
- Surprise readers with at least 3-4 counterintuitive or little-known facts about dog perception or behavior
- Present information visually and interactively (timelines, sense comparisons, life stage breakdowns) so it's more than a flat article

## Key questions Research must answer
1. How do dogs experience time — do they have a sense of duration, and why do they seem excited no matter how long you've been gone?
2. What does a typical dog's 24-hour day look like in terms of sleep, activity, and alertness cycles?
3. How powerful is a dog's sense of smell relative to humans, and what can they actually detect?
4. What are the key behavioral and physical milestones across a dog's life stages (puppy, adolescent, adult, senior)?
5. How do dogs communicate with each other and with humans through body language and vocalizations?
6. What are dogs' core social and emotional needs, and what happens when those needs go unmet?
7. How do different breeds vary in energy level, temperament, and cognitive style?

## Assumptions made
- Interpreted "dog life" as an exploration of what life is like for domestic dogs — their world, experience, and nature — rather than dog ownership tips or a specific breed focus.
- Assumed a general curious audience (not veterinarians or professional trainers).
- Focused on domestic dogs (Canis lupus familiaris) rather than wild canids.

## Open risks
- Some behavioral research (e.g., dog cognition, emotional capacity) is still evolving; Research should note where findings are contested or preliminary.
- "Dog life" could theoretically refer to slang ("living a dog life" = hard life) — dismissed as less likely given context.
