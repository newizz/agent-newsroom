## Headline findings
1. **Cats have 5 officially recognized life stages, not 2** — The 2021 AAHA/AAFP guidelines define Kitten, Young Adult, Mature Adult, Senior, and End-of-Life as distinct stages with different clinical and behavioral profiles.
2. **The meow was invented for humans** — Adult cats almost never meow to other cats; it is a behavior derived from kitten-to-mother mewing and redirected post-domestication specifically toward people.
3. **64–66% of cats are securely attached to their owners** — A 2019 Oregon State University study using the infant "strange situation test" found cats attach to human caregivers at nearly the same rate as human babies do to parents.
4. **The 2–7 week socialization window shapes personality for life** — Kittens handled by at least 4 different people before week 7 become measurably friendlier adults; most kittens are adopted only after this window has already closed.
5. **Neutering is the single largest controllable lifespan factor** — Neutered males live approximately 62% longer than intact males; spayed females live 39% longer than unspayed females.

## How to use the dashboard
Start with the life-stage arc at the top to orient yourself — each stage card shows the age range and dominant behavioral theme. Scroll into the milestone timeline to follow the week-by-week development of a kitten. The lifespan chart lets you compare indoor, outdoor, and neuter-status scenarios side by side. The "Surprising Facts" cards (meow, purr frequency, name recognition, CDS prevalence) are designed to be read in any order. The worked examples of "Bella," "Shadow," and "Miso" at the bottom show how the data plays out across different real-world cat lives.

## Limitations & caveats
- **Emotional and cognitive claims are partially preliminary.** Research into whether cats experience secondary emotions (guilt, jealousy, grief) is active and contested. The secure-attachment finding is solid; claims about richer inner states are less settled.
- **The purr-healing connection is suggestive, not proven in cats.** The 25–50 Hz bone-healing research involves external vibration in lab settings, not purring cats. The link is scientifically plausible but unconfirmed by controlled trials.
- **The "2–5 year outdoor cat lifespan" figure is hard to verify** at population scale, since street cat deaths are rarely formally recorded. Treat it as an order-of-magnitude range, not a precise statistic.
- **CDS prevalence figures** (55% at ages 11–15; 80% at 16–20) are widely cited but draw on studies with varied methodology. They are estimates, not precise survey results.
- **Breed and individual variation** is real but not covered — the dashboard treats "a typical domestic cat," which may not match highly specialized purebreds or cats with specific health conditions.
- All data reflects the state of feline research as of mid-2026; the field has been moving quickly since ~2018.
