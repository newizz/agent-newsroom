# Brief: Real Use Cases for Agentic AI in Various Industries

## Original prompt
> Real use cases for Agentic AI in various industry

## Refined question
What are the most concrete, real-world deployed applications of agentic AI (autonomous, multi-step AI systems) across different industries today, and what measurable outcomes or capability shifts do they produce?

## Scope
- **In scope:**
  - Definition of "agentic AI" distinguished from simple LLM chatbots (multi-step planning, tool use, autonomous action loops)
  - Real deployed use cases (not hypothetical), spanning at least 6-8 distinct industries
  - Industries to cover: healthcare, finance/banking, legal, software engineering/DevOps, manufacturing/supply chain, retail/e-commerce, customer service, research/pharma
  - Specific named products, companies, or documented pilots where possible
  - Types of agentic patterns in use: orchestrator-worker, RAG + action, human-in-the-loop, fully autonomous
  - Impact metrics where available (time saved, cost reduction, error rate, throughput)
  - Maturity spectrum: what's production-ready vs. early-stage pilot

- **Out of scope:**
  - General AI/ML (non-agentic) applications (e.g., image classifiers, recommendation engines)
  - Purely theoretical or academic research proposals
  - Consumer/personal AI assistants (Siri, Alexa) unless explicitly agentic
  - AGI or long-term speculative forecasts
  - Deep technical architecture of agentic frameworks (LangGraph, AutoGen, etc.) — only surface level

## Audience
Business decision-makers, product managers, and technical leads who are evaluating or already exploring AI adoption. They understand that AI exists but want concrete evidence of what agentic AI specifically delivers in production. They care about ROI, risk, and competitive differentiation — not just hype.

## Success criteria
A successful dashboard will:
- Give a clear, scannable map of agentic AI deployments across industries so the reader can quickly locate their own sector
- Provide at least one concrete named example per industry with enough detail to be credible (company, task automated, outcome)
- Distinguish agentic AI from standard AI automation so the reader understands what's genuinely new
- Show the maturity/readiness level per industry (early pilot vs. widespread production)
- Surface the most common agentic patterns (e.g., autonomous research loops, code generation + execution, multi-agent approval workflows) so practitioners can see cross-industry patterns
- Include at least some quantitative signals (time savings, throughput gains, cost metrics) to anchor the narrative in reality

## Key questions Research must answer
1. What is the clearest working definition of "agentic AI" that distinguishes it from earlier automation or simple LLM chatbots, and what are the 3-4 defining characteristics?
2. Which industries have the most mature / highest-volume production deployments of agentic AI as of 2025-2026, and what specific tasks are being automated?
3. For each major industry (healthcare, finance, legal, software engineering, manufacturing, retail, customer service, pharma/research): what are 1-3 real named deployments or documented pilots, and what outcomes have been reported?
4. What are the most common agentic workflow patterns appearing across industries (e.g., autonomous research → draft → review loops; plan → execute → verify coding agents; multi-agent approval chains)?
5. What measurable impact metrics have been publicly reported — time-to-completion, cost reduction, error rates, headcount equivalence?
6. What are the key risk factors and failure modes that have emerged in real deployments (hallucination, runaway agents, compliance issues, data privacy)?
7. Which industries or use cases are still in early/pilot stage versus those with proven, scaled production adoption?

## Assumptions made
- Assumed audience is professional/business-technical (not pure beginner, not pure researcher) — someone evaluating or implementing AI at an organization
- Assumed "various industry" means breadth over depth: cover 7-10 industries rather than deep-diving one
- Assumed "real use cases" means documented deployments or credible pilots, not vendor marketing claims without evidence
- Assumed current timeframe (2024-2026) is the focus — not historical AI applications

## Open risks
- Some deployments are proprietary and companies may not disclose details; Research may need to rely on press releases, analyst reports, or well-sourced journalism rather than academic papers
- "Agentic AI" is a marketing term as well as a technical term — Research should flag cases where vendor claims are questionable vs. genuinely multi-step autonomous systems
- Metrics vary wildly in how they're reported; apples-to-apples comparison across industries may not be possible — the dashboard should visualize ranges/qualitative maturity rather than forcing precise numbers
