# Brief: The History and Interesting Facts About Coffee

**Slug:** coffee-history-and-facts
**Created:** 2026-06-03
**Template hint:** timeline
**Ambiguity:** yellow
**Estimated depth:** standard

## Original prompt
> ความเป็นมา และอื่นๆ ที่น่าสนใจเกี่ยวกับกาแฟ

## Refined question
What is the origin and historical journey of coffee — from its discovery to its role in modern culture — and what are the most compelling facts that make coffee one of the world's most influential beverages?

## Scope
- **In scope:**
  - Origin story and early discovery of coffee (Ethiopia, 9th–10th century legend)
  - Spread through the Arab world, Ottoman Empire, and into Europe
  - Rise of coffeehouses as social and intellectual hubs
  - Coffee's global expansion and role in trade (colonial era, commodity markets)
  - Key milestones: invention of espresso, instant coffee, third-wave coffee movement
  - Fascinating facts: science of caffeine, global consumption statistics, coffee varieties (Arabica vs. Robusta), growing regions
  - Cultural significance across different countries

- **Out of scope:**
  - Detailed coffee brewing tutorials or recipes
  - Brand-specific histories (e.g., Starbucks corporate history)
  - Health/medical deep-dives beyond general caffeine facts
  - Coffee market financial analysis or investment perspectives

## Audience
General readers with casual-to-moderate curiosity about coffee — people who drink coffee daily but want to understand its story and significance. No prior historical or botanical knowledge assumed. They care about engaging narratives, surprising facts, and visual timelines they can explore at their own pace.

## Success criteria
A successful dashboard will:
- Present coffee's history as a clear, navigable chronological timeline from ancient origins to the modern era
- Surface 5–10 surprising or memorable facts that give readers new appreciation for their daily cup
- Balance historical narrative with visual fact highlights so both "story readers" and "fact skimmers" are served
- Feel authoritative yet approachable — informative without being academic

## Key questions Research must answer
1. What is the earliest credible evidence or legend of coffee's discovery, and where did it originate?
2. How did coffee spread from Ethiopia/Yemen to the Arab world, Europe, and eventually globally — and what were the key turning points?
3. What role did coffeehouses play in social and intellectual history (e.g., Enlightenment Europe, Ottoman culture)?
4. What are the most striking global consumption and production statistics today (top producing countries, cups consumed per day)?
5. What is the difference between Arabica and Robusta, and why does it matter to the coffee we drink?
6. What are the most surprising or counterintuitive facts about coffee (biology, chemistry, culture, economics)?
7. How has coffee culture evolved in the modern era — from espresso bars to third-wave specialty coffee?

## Assumptions made
- The Thai prompt ("ความเป็นมา และอื่นๆ ที่น่าสนใจ") implies a broad, entertaining treatment rather than a scholarly deep-dive; depth is set to "standard" accordingly.
- The intended output language for the dashboard is English (consistent with project conventions), though Thai readers are a likely audience.
- "Interesting things" (อื่นๆ ที่น่าสนใจ) is interpreted as fascinating facts, trivia, and cultural highlights — not opinion or recommendations.
- Template chosen is `timeline` (over `concept-explorer`) because the historical spine ("ความเป็นมา" = origin/history) is the primary structure; fact highlights will be layered as callout panels within the timeline rather than requiring a separate template.

## Open risks
- The topic is extremely broad; without a depth cap, research could balloon. Mitigation: cap timeline to ~8–10 key eras/milestones.
- Some origin stories (e.g., the Kaldi goat-herder legend) are folklore, not verified history — Research must distinguish legend from documented fact.
- "Interesting facts" is subjective; the selection should prioritize globally surprising facts over region-specific ones to serve a general audience.
- Thai-language sources may exist for this topic but English sources will dominate — any cultural nuance relevant to Thai coffee culture (e.g., Thai iced coffee traditions) may be under-represented.
